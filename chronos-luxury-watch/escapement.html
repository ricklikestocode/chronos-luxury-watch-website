Escapement

The regulator of mechanical time

The escapement is one of the most critical mechanisms inside a mechanical watch. It controls how energy stored in the mainspring is released throughout the movement.

Without the escapement, the energy stored inside the mainspring would release all at once. The escapement regulates this energy, allowing it to move through the watch in small, controlled intervals.

This controlled release of energy produces the familiar "tick-tock" sound of a mechanical watch. Each tick represents a tiny release of energy through the escapement.

The escapement works closely with the balance wheel. While the balance wheel oscillates back and forth, the escapement locks and unlocks the movement to maintain a consistent rhythm.

Watchmakers spend significant time perfecting the escapement because it directly affects the accuracy and reliability of the watch.

In luxury mechanical watches like Chronos, the escapement is engineered with extreme precision to ensure smooth operation and long-term durability. It is considered one of the greatest achievements in mechanical engineering.
