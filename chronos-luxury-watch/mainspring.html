Mainspring

The power source of the mechanical movement

The mainspring is the primary power source of a mechanical watch. It stores energy that is gradually released to drive the entire watch movement.

When the watch is wound, either manually or through an automatic rotor, the mainspring tightens inside a barrel. This process stores mechanical energy within the coiled spring.

As the mainspring slowly unwinds, it releases that stored energy in a controlled manner. This energy flows through the gear train, powers the escapement, and ultimately drives the hands of the watch.

The quality of the mainspring directly affects the power reserve of the watch. High-quality mainsprings can store energy efficiently and maintain consistent torque throughout the unwinding process.

In luxury watches like Chronos, the mainspring is engineered using advanced alloys to ensure long-lasting durability and reliable power delivery.

Thanks to the mainspring, a mechanical watch can operate continuously for many hours or even days without needing to be wound again. It is the foundation upon which all mechanical timekeeping is built.
