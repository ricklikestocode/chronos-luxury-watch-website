Mechanical Movement Architecture

A mechanical watch movement is a living system of springs, gears, and balance wheels working in perfect synchronization. Each gear tooth transfers controlled energy through the movement, regulating time with extraordinary precision. The Chronos movement reveals the discipline of mechanical engineering — where friction, geometry, and material science combine to produce accuracy that lasts generations.

The exposed mechanism allows observers to appreciate the harmony of rotating wheels, jewel bearings, and the oscillating balance spring — the heart of the watch.

Mechanical architecture rendered with quiet precision

Movement Components

The essential systems behind every measured second

Gear Train

Transfers stored energy through finely calculated ratios to regulate motion across the calibre.

Balance Wheel

The oscillating heart of the movement, maintaining rhythm, stability, and timing precision.

Escapement

Releases energy in exact increments, converting stored force into disciplined mechanical timekeeping.

Mainspring

Stores the power reserve and drives the movement through a gradual, controlled unwinding cycle.
