Balance Wheel

The heart of the mechanical movement

The balance wheel is often referred to as the "heart" of a mechanical watch. It regulates the flow of energy through the movement and ensures that the watch keeps accurate time.

Inside the watch, the balance wheel oscillates back and forth at a constant rhythm. This oscillation controls how energy from the mainspring is released through the escapement.

Each swing of the balance wheel represents a precise measurement of time. In many mechanical watches, the balance wheel oscillates several times per second, creating the familiar ticking sound associated with traditional watchmaking.

Luxury watchmakers carefully adjust the balance wheel to achieve perfect accuracy. Even tiny changes in weight or shape can influence the performance of the entire movement.

In high-end mechanical watches like Chronos, the balance wheel is finely tuned so the watch can maintain precise timekeeping under different conditions such as temperature changes or daily wear.

Because of its importance, the balance wheel is one of the most fascinating and delicate parts of a mechanical watch movement. It represents centuries of engineering refinement and continues to be the cornerstone of precision timekeeping.
